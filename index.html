Remedy

Software Used: Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign, Animate, After Effects, & Fresco



For my college thesis project, I developed a brand called Remedy aimed at supporting individuals in overcoming childhood emotional neglect through monthly care-packages filled with tools for self-discovery and healing. These packages include informative booklets, self-guided journals (offering variations like mindfulness, creative, and active journals), self-care items, and a positive affirming calendar. The design radiates warmth and sincerity, creating a welcoming environment conducive to growth. Every aspect, from carefully selected color schemes to typography and illustrations, is intentionally crafted to evoke a sense of safety and encouragement for those on their journey to emotional well-being.

Niagara Nutrition Partners

Software Used: Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, Animate, & After Effects



Collaborating with a fellow graphic designer, I had the privilege to revamp print and digital materials for Niagara Nutrition Partners, a non-profit organization dedicated to fostering success through nourishment. At the heart of this rebranding endeavor was a powerful message: the foundation of success is built on good food. This message shaped every design decision and was evident in every facet of the project.

Ok Orchestra Album Cover Redesign

Software Used: Adobe Illustrator & Photoshop



The redesign of the album cover for AJR's "Ok Orchestra" showcases a fusion of multimedia skills, blending acrylic and watercolor paints with vector graphics. Each character was crafted with acrylics, while the backdrop exudes the fluidity of watercolors. Embracing maximalism, the cover art reflects the whirlwind of emotions and anxieties inherent in the journey to adulthood. To harmonize with the vibrant imagery, a custom hand-rendered typeface was crafted, adding a feeling that resonates with the album's themes and artistic vision.

Canadian Symbol Stamp Design

Software Used: Adobe Illustrator & Photoshop



Through this project, I delved deeper into Canada's wildlife and cultural heritage, particularly focusing on Algonquin National Park and its resident pine martens. My task involved crafting a stamp design for Canada Post, starting with hand-sketched pine marten and transitioning it into a digital format. In this digital realm, I incorporated various graphic elements and vibrant colors, drawing inspiration from beautiful Canadian imagery such as the Aurora Borealis. By infusing elements of Canadian culture into the design, I aimed to capture the essence of the park and its inhabitants while paying homage to the country's rich natural and cultural landscapes.
