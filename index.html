Niagara Nutrition Partners

In this project I had the opportunity to collaborate with a fellow graphic designer to create print and digital assets for the non profit organization Niagara Nutrition Partners. The main message behind this rebrand is that the foundation of success is built on good food. This was the basis of all the design choices and was reflected throughout the entire project.

Remedy

In this project I created a brand for Remedy who deals with healing childhood trauma, more specifically overcoming childhood emotional neglect. Remedy offers a monthly care-package that provides various tools to help individuals overcome their past trauma by learning about their emotions and feelings.
The brand was meant to feel welcoming and warm as to encourage growth and healing, but remain serious and sincere. Many design choices were made to reflect this idea, including colour, typeface, graphic elements and illustration style. There is also a pattern reflected throughout the entire box that changes with each month.

Stamp design

In this project I got to explore more of Canada’s wildlife and culture by learning about Algonquin National Park and the pine martens that live there.
To create this stamp design for Canada post, I sketched a pine marten by hand then converted it to a digital setting. This is where I added many other graphic elements and colours. I used different aspects of Canadian culture to create this final design including the Aurora Borealis and their colours.

dinosaur playing cards

The purpose of this project was to create my own playing cards that were more than just your traditional cards. I decided to create dinosaur playing cards that were split into four different suits: herbivores, carnivores, aquatic dinosaurs, and flying dinosaurs. They were designed in a way that they could be used as regular playing cards, but could also be used for a more engaging educational experience. Each of the face cards included a dinosaur illustration and information behind each of these dinosaurs.

bearly surviving

This infographic poster was designed to present cross referenced data and statistics in a new and engaging way. The goal of the design was to have hidden graphical elements so that it didn't feel like you were looking at much information.
The mountain is a dot plot graph where each bear is a point on the scale. Each bear themselves was also a graph. They were to represent parts of a whole and are coloured into a percentage of their size to represent another set of data.
